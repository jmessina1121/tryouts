IINF 362 - Intermediate Interactive Design

Course Information

A technique-oriented intermediate exploration of client-based and server-based Web design and development technologies, using current and emerging technologies. Design, planning, security and management of websites will also be examined.

Working in teams students will collaborate to design and develop an accessible, responsive website/app for a client. Teams will be working with students from Albany Law School to build a website for an agency.

Due to the nature of the course, the schedule will constantly be adjusting to coordinate with the Albany Law students. The course will add and remove topics based on the interest and level of the students in the course.

Prerequisite

The prerequisite course for IINF 362 is ICSI 101, 105, 110, or 201, IINF 201.

The primary concepts from the prerequisite course that are relevant in this course are HTML and CSS. This course will build on these concepts and add several more.
